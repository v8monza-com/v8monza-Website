What does your trim tag tell you?


The above pictured tag can be found, near the drivers side hood hinge in the engine compartment, above the firewall.
Although the one pictured is for a camaro. See below for what would have come on a typical 78 monza with the "spyder" option...

ST 78 1HR07 U 344361 BDY
TR 19V A51 77L 77U PNT
05B. 19X. 69-3681
191601 . . 02BLK.


For 1978 cars..
from above example left to right..top to bottom
78= model year..1978
1=Chevy
H= platform..as in "H-body"
R= Sport Model..(square headlite version as opposed to round headlite)
07=hatchback
U= assembly plant......U=Lordstown OH. assy.
344361= number assigned to the body when it was a metal shell.started at 000001 every model year I believe.
19V= black vinyl seats
A51=bucket seats
77L=paint color..77 is carmine met.
77U= paint color again.
05B.=date code..05 is the fifth month..May...B =second week of that month
19X.=black carpets floorpan and hatch area
69=169th day of production* if the date code was from around november than it would have indicated the 69th day of production..
3681= ?? dont know but this # rose by about 2000 a day continuosly throughout the production cycle..think they made 2000 h-bodys a day??? It's a possability but i dont think so...let me know if you know what this # was counting!
191601**the 91,601st monza. in a series of of 138,832 built for 1978
02BLK**..... 02= Z02 package BLK either meant black stripes or black highlights/trim where applicable.
**= in 1979 the vin number was not stamped in anymore ..and Z02 equipped spyders may have only had an 02 with no letters for stripe color as in 02BLK.
