  Replacement V8 Motor Mounts
These motor mounts have been specially designed and hand-made by Bob Gumm to replace your factory V8 mounts.   Factory mounts are no longer available and even if you can find them you'll pay over $200.00 for them.   Our mounts were designed using factory mounts as a template and with future replacement costs in mind.   After just a few months original mounts begin to sag, but not our mounts.   Our mounts are designed using an off-the-shelf mount that by design resists sagging.   When these mounts finally do need replacement, you can pick up a replacement mount at your local parts store for about $10 or less.   Take a look at this photo of our mounts.   Remember, because these mounts maintain their original form they'll give you back the clearance between your oil pan and K-frame/steering components.   Some customers have reported reduced clearance between the engine and hood with these mounts.   Not that our mounts are taller, it's just that the original mounts sag so badly that the engine eventually sat lower.   Some folks who had the old sagging mounts had installed performance induction systems that just cleared the hood.   Our mounts bring the engine back up to where it should be and as a result there is less clearance between some induction systems and the hood.   These mounts are available for immediate shipment.

Sanderson CC13 Headers
Plain painted (P) these headers are made to fit your standard Chevy heads (R).   These are shorty headers for your GM H-Body.   They'll work with just about any engine mounting system available.   This means you can use them with the factory V8 mounts, Don Hardy, Herbert-Meek/Hurst, Hooker, Motion Performance, Trans-dapt, and similar kits.   They're designed with extra-thick exhaust port flanges for the best seal we've ever seen to combat the typical annoying header leak!   They're a one-piece design which further prevents exhaust leaks that are common with multi-piece headers.   This one-piece design means that the steering column must be temporarily removed to install the left (driver's side) header since one tube goes around the steering column.   Sanderson also recommends you inspect your engine mounts, header bolts, and header to steering column clearance regularly.   This is to prevent the header from contacting the steering column which could cause steering interference during vehicle operation.   These parts are shipped directly from Sanderson.   Sometimes stock runs out because of unforeseeable events.   Because of this some items may not ship immediately and can take up to 2-4 weeks for shipping.
