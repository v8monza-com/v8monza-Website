GM H-Body V8 Cooling FAQ
(frequently asked questions)
By Bob Gumm

    The H-Body cars (Chevrolet Vega, Monza, Spyder, and Mirage, Buick Skyhawk, Roadhawk, and Nighthawk, Oldsmobile Starfire, and Firenza, as well as Pontiac Astre, Sunbird, and Formula) have a lot in common and can share a lot of the V6 and V8 H-Body parts.   Since this site's focus is mainly V8s, and V8 cooling is typically the hardest to tackle, I'll cover only that information.   If you have a V6, you'll find many of the tips here will still come in handy, just use your own judgement.   All H-Bodies are relatively the same except for the Chevy Vega or Pontiac Astre, as they both use a shorter height factory radiator.   There are two ways to make a raditor fit a Chevy Vega or Pontiac Astre, modify the lower mounts to use a taller radiator or use a purpose built racing radiator. If you're looking for cooling tips and tricks, it's all here on v8monza.com.

Bob Gumm

H-Body Engine Cooling Factors
Radiator
Size/Construction 		Water
Flow/Quantity/Quality 		Air
Flow/Temperature/Density
Large/2-3 Core/Low Fin Count High/Large/Low Tension High/Low/High
Medium/1-2 Core/Med Fin Count Medium/Medium/Medium Medium/Medium/Medium
Small/1 Core/High Fin Count Low/Small/High Tension Low/High/Low
        As can be seen in the table above, the recipe for optimum cooling is a radiator with a large surface area and low fin count (to promote better air flow), a large quantity of water at high flow and low surface tension, and high flow of low temperature/high density air.

  Radiator Size and Construction

V8 Radiator
Factory Radiator
    The factory V8 Monza radiator is a 3 core unit that is 16.5" tall (3 inches higher than a 4 cylinder Vega single core unit) and it has an irregular tank that makes it 25.25" wide at the bottom and 26.25" wide at the top.   The core is 21" wide.   Note that factory V8 Monzas used the short style high flow Corvette water pumps with a larger ?/?" dia. pump shaft instead of the more common ?/?".

    The V8 Monza radiator requires modification to tackle engine cooling problems on V8 Vegas by relocating the upper outlet to the Vega location and mounting it 3 inches lower, requiring relocated lower radiator brackets on the Vega.   Installing a larger radiator will also require a larger fan.   Be sure to check fan shroud and radiator clearances before starting the engine, no sense in trashing a new fan or radiator.   When looking in the factory parts manuals for these, you'll only find them in Group 1.219 of the Chevrolet Parts Catalog Models thru 1975 (Chevrolet Parts Publication, Book No. 11 and 11A).
